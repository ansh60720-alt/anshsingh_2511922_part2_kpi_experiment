## Quick Summary

I looked at the data from the experiment we did. The new onboarding campaign is really good at getting people to pay for our product. It more than doubled the number of people who paid for our product. This is not luck. The numbers are real.

I also saw some problems. More people are asking for help. The money we get from each person who pays is less. Because of these problems I think we should only launch the campaign for some groups of people. We should watch what happens before we launch it for everyone.

## 1. Business Problem

Our company tried something to see if it would work better than what we were doing before. We split the users into two groups:

- One group got the old experience

- The other group got the new campaign

The big question is: should we launch the new campaign for all new users?

This decision affects the users, the people who help our customers and the people who make sure we make money.

Before I could make a recommendation I needed to answer some questions:

- Is the campaign really better? → Yes

- Are all the important numbers okay? → Not all of them

- Does the campaign work for all groups of people? → No

---

## 2. What we want to achieve with the campaign

**We want more people to pay for our product**

I chose this because the new campaign is supposed to get more people to pay for our product. If people do not pay we do not make money.

Other numbers, like how many people use our product are important.. They are not the most important thing. The important thing is getting people to pay for our product.

**What could go wrong if we only look at this:**

If we only care about getting people to pay we might get people who pay and then ask for their money back.. They might not use our product much. This would make our numbers look good. It would hurt our company.

## 8. What I recommend

**Launch the campaign for some groups of people and watch what happens**

I do not think we should launch the new campaign for everyone.. The new campaign is too good to ignore.

**What I recommend:**

1. Launch the campaign for people who use mobile devices come from referral and paid search live in the North, South and East and choose the free plan. These groups do well with the campaign and have fewer problems.

2. Do not launch the campaign for people who come from social media and live in the West. The new campaign does not work well for them.

3. Watch what happens to the number of people who ask for help and the number of people who ask for their money back. If the number of people who ask for help gets too high we might need to stop the launch.

4. Figure out why we make money from each person who pays for our product. Is it because people choose plans? Do people stop using our product

5. Make a version of the new campaign for people who come from social media because the current version does not work well for them.
